decksh can read Shapefiles, KML, or geoJSON encoded data to produce geometric regions, borders and paths.
Plain and labeled locations are also supported along with images at geographic locations. 
\n
The keywords for geo functions are `geoborder' (geographic borders), 'georegion' (regions), 'geopath' (paths between points), various labels and markers ('geoloc', 'geolabel', 'geomark').
\n
To place a map using GIS data in KML format:
\n
georegion "africa.kml"
\n
Geographic locations may be assigned to variables using geo URL syntax, followed by an optional <tab> and label. For example Los Angeles is defined as:
\n
losangeles="geo:34.05,-118.25	Los Angeles"
\n
The geographic bounding box of a map is defined by latitude (-90° to 90°) and longitude (-180° to 180°) in decimal degrees.
\n
The 'geobound' keyword maps the specified lat/long boundaries to the slide canvas boundaries set with the 'geocanvas' keywords. The defaults for geobound are -90 90 -180 180, with geocanvas defaulting to 0 100 0 100 (geoXmin, geoXmax, geoYmin, geoYmax).
