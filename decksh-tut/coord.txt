Decksh uses the traditional Cartesian coordinate system
to place elements on the canvas.  The x, y coordinates represent percentages ranging from 0-100;
the coordinates are passed as arguments as a pair of numbers, x first, then y.
\n
The origin (0,0), is at the lower-left, with the x coordinate increasing from left to right, with the y coordinate increasing bottom to top. The middle of the canvas is (50,50): half-way from the left and half-way up from the bottom.
\n
For example, to place the text "Hello" left-aligned at 20% from the left, and 30% up (20,30), and the text size at 5% of the canvas width, use:
\n
text "Hello" 20 30 5
\n
Other elements like lines and circles may also be placed. For example
\n
circle 50 80 5 "red"
\n
places a red circle at (50,80), the size of the circle is 5% of the canvas width.
\n
To draw a line from (60,10) to (90,60)
\n
line 60 10 90 60
\n
Finally, note the `ruler' keyword, which makes a convenient scale.
