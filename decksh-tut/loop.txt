Loops allow you repeat decksh operations in a contolled way.
\n
A loop is defined as statements bounded by the 'for' and 'efor' keywords.
The 'for' keyword arguments define the beginning, ending, and optional increment.
This defines how many times the statements between 'for' and 'efor' are executed.
The variable specified in the 'for' statement is substituted in the these statements.
For example:
\n
for v=10 50
\n
assigns the value 'v' from 10 to 50, with an increment of 1.
\n
for v=10 50 10
\n
counts from 10 to 50 by 10 (10, 20, 30, 40, 50).
\n
