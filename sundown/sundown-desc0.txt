“Sundown, sunset, or ‘gray’ towns emerged in the 1800s, enforcing racial exclusion through social boundaries, jurisdictional control, signage, violence, threats, and local community enforcement. Though often absent from official records, evidence from community memory, archives, and research suggests thousands of U.S. places practiced these forms of exclusion. Preserving and sharing these histories reveals how past spatial exclusion still shapes segregation, resources, and health care access today."
